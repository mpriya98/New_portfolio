Outbound engagement tracking

Community managers leave hundreds of comments a week on viral posts, trending threads, and creator content — building brand presence one interaction at a time. It's one of the most active parts of social strategy. And until now, one of the least measurable. Outbound Engagement made it trackable: a dashboard where every comment your team places can be quantified, reported on, and acted on.

Collaborators Rhea (Designer) Tushar Beladiya (Engineer) Lap Nguyen (Engineer)
Timeline 2 months
My Role Designed the outbound tracking dashboard and the comment upload flow and navigate the AI verification problem in the upload experience.

Impact!

Outbound wasn't just a new dashboard — it gave community teams a language to talk about work that had always been invisible. When it shipped, the response from community managers, social strategists, and brand teams was immediate.

The comment metrics that went unmeasured

A single comment on a viral post can pull in hundreds of thousands of likes, replies, impressions and six figures in EMV. That performance existed all along. It just had nowhere to be measured.

Likes

Replies

EMV

The outbound dashboard

The main dashboard gives community managers and their leads a single view of every comment the team has placed—across platforms, across time. At its core, it is proof of the value of community engagement and the impact of a brand's outbound strategy.

The dashboard consists of 4 parts:

  • Community Growth Report — top comments, metrics, and leaderboard, auto-compiled into a downloadable PDF.
  • Metrics over time — comments, likes, replies, impressions, and EMV across a custom date range, with daily breakdowns on hover.
  • Brand's comments — the brand's best-performing comments in one searchable, exportable feed.
  • Community leaderboard — every manager ranked by engagement, volume, and score, filterable by topic, platform, and date.

Community growth report - the most loved piece!

Think Spotify Wrapped for the most unhinged comments. Weekly, monthly, or yearly — top comments, key metrics, and team leaderboard compiled automatically and downloadable as a PDF. Built so team members have their performance visible across the team without anyone having to ask.

How comments get into the dashboard

Not every comment gets placed from the same context. The team might be monitoring through Plot's feed, commenting from their phone, or uploading a batch after the fact. The product supports all three paths.

Auto
Via social listening

Plot's listening pipeline auto-detects comments placed by tracked handles. Nothing to upload — it just shows up.

Upload
Comment URLs

Paste a post link from desktop. AI extracts the comment data automatically.

Manual
Comment screenshot entry

Drop in a screenshot when the AI can't detect a comment by URL. Best for one-offs or edge cases the link parser misses.

Auto-tracking via social listening

When a tracked handle leaves a comment, Plot surfaces it in the social listening feed with a quick action to log it directly to the outbound dashboard. No copy-pasting, no context switching.

Uploading single or bulk URLs

Teams can paste one comment URL or drop in a batch at once. Plot parses each link and pulls the comment data automatically, so a week's worth of outbound work can be logged in one pass.

Manually adding comment screenshot

When a comment can't be found by URL, teams can drop in a screenshot instead. Plot extracts the comment data straight from the image.

Upload with verification step

The AI processes the screenshot and surfaces what it extracted — comment text, metrics, the post it came from. Users confirm or correct before anything is logged. The step exists because hallucinations happen, and catching them early stops bad data from compounding.

Behind the scenes

Reflections

From the start, we knew we didn't want to build just another dashboard. We were designing for social media teams, so it needed to be both valuable and delightful to use. One of the most rewarding parts of the project was shaping the Community Growth Report, an experience that felt insightful, shareable, and fun.

Along the way, seemingly simple inputs like URLs and screenshots revealed various edge cases. Designing AI-powered products reinforced that edge cases exist everywhere—in the technology, the data, and the ways people interact with a product. Every release uncovers new behaviors, assumptions, and opportunities to learn. Building the product is only half the challenge; understanding how people actually use it is the other half.
